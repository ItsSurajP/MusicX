F1 2021 Tracks: Singapore GP cancelled, 21 circuits confirmed on launch, post-release DLC brings three new tracks

The new F1 2021 game is speeding towards its release date.

Codemasters has lifted the curtain on some details of the game, including what tracks will be available in the official Formula 1 game.

Thanks to covid, the F1 calendar has been a fluid and ever-changing thing which certainly makes life difficult for Coders.

So what circuits will they have on offer for players in F1 2021?

Latest news – Istanbul Park returns (again)

The vacant weekend left by the withdrawal of Singapore this year has been filled by a visit to the always wonderful Istanbul Park.

Running from 1-3 October, the Turkish Grand Prix will form the second race of a triple-header that now sees the teams go from Sochi in Russia, across to Turkey, and then all the way out to Japan!

While the Turkish GP is a great addition for fans, it won’t be in the F1 2021 game.

New tracks DLC

Because of all the calendar changes, the exact 2021 calendar of tracks will not be available straight off the bat for players.

However, in a free post-launch DLC Codemasters will be providing Imola, Portimao, and Jeddah for players.

How these then integrate with Career Mode or My Team saves that are already underway isn’t quite clear. Both modes will have a full, medium, and short-season option, but word on a fully-customisable calendar has not yet come through.

China included

The vacant weekend left by the withdrawal of Singapore this year has been filled by a visit to the always wonderful Istanbul Park.

However, Codemasters had already created their Braking Point story, which included the Chinese Grand Prix.

As a result, China will be in F1 2021, just not in the usual way. It won’t be present in the Season 1 calendar of My Team or Career Mode. However, from Season 2 and beyond it will be.

It will also be immediately raceable on Time Trial and Multiplayer, meaning you can get it into your League right away.

Short tracks

While unconfirmed, we expect the four short variants to once again be present in F1 2021.

That’s Bahrain, Great Britain, USA, and Japan.
